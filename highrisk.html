Frequently Asked Questions

What makes this the "high risk" page?

This page tracks the same setups as the standard page, but it only books a win when price reaches Target 2 — the second, further profit goal. Because the target is further away, trades stay open longer and a larger share can be stopped out before getting there. The reward is bigger, but so is the risk. The Standard (Target 1) page books a win at the closer Target 1.

How is the Target 2 success rate calculated?

The Target 2 Success Rate is the percentage of T2-resolved signals that reached Target 2 — that is, signals that either hit T2 (success) or were stopped out before reaching it (failure). Trades still working toward Target 2 are not counted until they resolve. The rate is not a guarantee, and past performance is not a reflection of future performance.

What do "T1" and "T2" mean?

T1 (Target 1) and T2 (Target 2) are the two profit objectives for a trade. A green check ✅ appears next to a target once price has reached it. "Stop" is the stop-loss level where the setup is considered invalidated.

What does SHORT vs LONG mean?

LONG expects the price to rise (targets are above entry). SHORT expects the price to fall (targets are below entry). The badge next to each ticker shows the direction.

How often is the site updated?

The tables are updated at the end of each trading day. New setups are marked with a gold "NEW" badge and show the date they were opened.
